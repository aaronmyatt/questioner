{%- comment -%} Injected into by just-the-docs. Goal: kill the per-navigation flicker of a classic multi-page site. Two parts: 1. View Transitions (CSS) — cross-fade between same-origin pages instead of a hard white-flash reload. Crucially we SCOPE it: the sidebar and header are identical between pages, so fading them is just visual noise. Naming them lifts them out of the transition and `animation: none` holds them static — only the content area cross-fades. 2. Speculation Rules (prerender) — the browser prerenders likely next pages on hover, so activating them is near-instant. This removes the click→paint latency that a cross-fade alone can't hide. Both are progressive enhancement: browsers without support (Firefox as of 2026 for both; Safari supports view transitions 18.2+) just fall back to normal navigation. just-the-docs' own JS is untouched because navigation stays full-page. Refs: https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/View_Transition_API https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/API/Speculation_Rules_API {%- endcomment -%}
